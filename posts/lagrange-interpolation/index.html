Lagrange Interpolation

2022-09-14
0 minute read
ml

A special class of models is polynomial: $$ h_\theta(x) = \theta_d x^d + \theta_{d - 1}x^{d - 1} + \cdots + \theta_1x + \theta_0. $$ Fitting a polynomial: Lagrange interpolation produces a perfect fit, i.e. $$ \begin{align*} L(x) &= \sum^n_{i=1} y_i \ell_i(x) \ \ell_i &= \prod_{0 \le m \le n, m \ne i} \frac{x - x_m}{x_i - x_m} \ L(x_i) &= y_i, \forall i \in [n]. \end{align*} $$ The advantage of Lagrange interpolation is that no training is required. All we need to do is to write down the $L$.
